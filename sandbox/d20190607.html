Uncertainty propagation¶

The variations of different uncertainty sources as a function of $p_{\rm T}$ in 2.5 < $y$ < 4 are shown in figure 40 in the analysis note. The trend should be similar in each sub-rapidity bin.

Since I do not have all ingredients, two sources are chosen to test the uncertainty propagation:

  • Systematic uncertainty on background subtraction
  • Systematic uncertainty on detector response

With the strategy described by Zuman, the value of systematic uncertainty on background subtraction in the total rapidity bin should be the algebra (linear) sum of that in sub-rapidities. Since it is dominant at low-$p_{\rm T}$, the relation should be well kept in this region.

For the uncertainty on detector response, its relative value is correlated with both $p_{\rm T}$ and rapidity in each muon trigger sample. It means, similar as uncertainty on background subtraction, that the value of this uncertainty in total rapidity bin is given by the algebra sum of that in sub-rapidities.

In region of 2 < $p_{\rm T}$ < 6 GeV/$c$, the uncertainty on background subtraction is dominant at low-$p_{\rm T}$ and uncertainty on detector response is dominant at high-$p_{\rm T}$. In between, the two uncertainties should be added quadratically during the propagation, hence interfering each other. To make ratio of algebra sum of systematic uncertainties in sub-rapidities and the systematic uncertainty in the total rapidity bin, the expected trend of this ratio in 2 < $p_{\rm T}$ < 6 should be unity at $p_{\rm T}$ = 2 GeV/$c$, then start to deviate and go back to unity at the end. However, this is not what is observed in the blue points in the left figure.

The ratio of the quadratic sum of systematic uncertainties in sub-rapidities and the systematic uncertainty in total rapidity bin is shown as the yellow points in the left figure. The value would achieve to 1 / $\sqrt{5}$ if the correlated uncertainty in each sub-rapidity bin has the same (absolute) value. The results show that this limit is approached at intermediate-$p_{\rm T}$ and this should NOT be the case!

Another observation in the left figure is that the shapes of the ratios with the linear sum and the quadratic sum are very similar. The corresponding ratio is shown in the right figure. The values are around 2 and almost independent on $p_{\rm T}$. How can this be!!!
